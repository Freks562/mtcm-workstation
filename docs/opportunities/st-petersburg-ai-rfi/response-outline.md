# City of St. Petersburg AI RFI — Response Outline

## 1. Executive response angle

MTCM proposes a practical, human-in-control approach to AI-assisted municipal operations focused on reducing employee workload, improving service quality, and preserving accountable human decision-making while aligning implementation to governance, security, and public-records obligations.

## 2. Opportunity summary

- City of St. Petersburg is evaluating AI for internal operations, public-facing services, data analysis, and workflow automation.
- This is treated as a near-term municipal pursuit in active repo truth.
- Response approach should remain operational and compliance-first, not workforce-replacement framing.

## 3. Why this fits MTCM

- Aligns with current practical positioning around workload relief and service quality.
- Supports human oversight and accountability in public operations.
- Fits current consolidation direction: controlled, evidence-based capability positioning.

## 4. Proposed response structure

1. Cover/intent letter
2. Understanding of City goals and constraints
3. Human-in-control operating model
4. Priority municipal workflow use cases (initial candidates)
5. Governance/security/public-records control approach
6. Delivery approach (pilot, evaluation, scale criteria)
7. Assumptions, dependencies, and open questions
8. Attachments and supporting references

## 5. Suggested positioning statement

“MTCM is interested in AI-assisted municipal operations that reduce employee workload, improve service quality, and preserve human decision-making, with strong attention to governance, security, and public-records compliance.”
