# City of St. Petersburg AI RFI — First Draft Response (Internal Review)

## Draft control

- **Document status:** Internal draft for review
- **Prepared for:** MTCM internal pursuit review
- **Prepared on:** 2026-04-17
- **Scope note:** This is a docs-only drafting artifact and not a submitted response.

## 1) Cover intent (draft)

MTCM appreciates the City of St. Petersburg’s request for information regarding AI use in municipal operations. Our approach is practical, compliance-first, and human-in-control: we focus on reducing administrative burden, improving service quality, and preserving accountable human decision-making in City workflows.

## 2) Understanding of City objectives (draft)

Based on current pursuit notes, the City is exploring AI support across internal operations, public-facing services, data analysis, and workflow automation.

**Assumption A-01 (unconfirmed):** The City intends this RFI as market research for phased implementation planning rather than immediate full-scale procurement.

## 3) MTCM operating position (draft)

MTCM proposes AI-assisted workflows where:

- AI contributes recommendations, summaries, and process acceleration.
- City personnel retain final authority for decisions, approvals, and citizen-impacting outcomes.
- Governance, records, and security requirements are built into workflow design from the start.

## 4) Human-in-control model (draft)

Key control elements:

1. **Decision rights:** Human reviewers maintain final approval authority.
2. **Escalation:** Uncertain/high-risk outputs are routed to designated City reviewers.
3. **Auditability:** Inputs, outputs, reviewer actions, and final decisions are traceable.
4. **Fallback:** Manual procedures remain available when AI assistance is unavailable or unsuitable.

## 5) Initial use-case categories (draft)

Use-case categories should prioritize measurable workload relief and service impact:

- Internal administrative workflow support
- Public inquiry triage and response support
- Document/data summarization and pattern discovery
- Staff workflow routing and task coordination support

**Assumption A-02 (unconfirmed):** Final use-case priorities will be set by City stakeholders after clarification of departmental needs and evaluation criteria.

## 6) Governance, security, and public-records alignment (draft)

MTCM’s draft response posture is to align with City requirements for:

- retention and records handling,
- auditable decision processes,
- access control and least privilege,
- exception and incident handling,
- clear policy ownership and operating accountability.

**Assumption A-03 (unconfirmed):** Specific control language and evidence format must be aligned to the final official RFI/addenda package.

## 7) Delivery approach (draft)

Proposed phased model:

1. **Discovery and controls alignment**
2. **Pilot implementation in selected workflows**
3. **Evaluation against agreed metrics**
4. **Scale decision with governance checkpoints**

**Assumption A-04 (unconfirmed):** The City is open to pilot-first delivery rather than requiring a fully scaled design at initial response stage.
